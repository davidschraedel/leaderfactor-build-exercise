# Product Requirements Document

Version: 1.0

## Problem Statement

LeaderFactor learners complete training and create action plans — concrete behavioral commitments tied to assessment results — but fewer than 5% continue engaging with those plans after training ends. The current twice-weekly reminder emails are passive and easy to ignore, so consistent practice never becomes a habit. Without sustained engagement, behavior change stalls, managers stay uninformed about what their direct reports committed to, and L&D admins lack sellable proof that training investment is driving development between assessment cycles.

This prototype replaces the broken reminder flow with a frictionless weekly check-in habit loop, a lightweight biweekly manager-awareness layer, and a simple admin visibility view — forming a coherent post-training habit-builder that supports LeaderFactor's assess → learn → apply → prove model without rebuilding the full platform.

## Target Users

- **Primary:** Learners who have completed a LeaderFactor training and committed to practicing one specific behavior over a ~90-day cycle.
- **Secondary:** People managers whose direct reports are in an active action-plan cycle and who need lightweight awareness (not a new dashboard) of team progress and commitments.
- **Secondary:** L&D admins who purchase trainings and need admin-visible engagement metrics to demonstrate program value and justify spend.
- **NOT For:** Facilitators running live cohort sessions, IT/security teams evaluating enterprise SSO or compliance, learners who haven't completed training or created a commitment, or organizations seeking a full LMS replacement or persistent learner/manager portal.

## Goals and Success Metrics

- **Weekly check-in rate:** Percentage of active learners who respond to their weekly prompt (target: materially above the current \<5% post-training engagement baseline).
- **Reporting consistency:** Average weeks reported per learner over a 90-day cycle (target: majority of learners report in ≥75% of eligible weeks).
- **Reporting pattern visibility:** Share of learners with visible week-by-week history showing sustained check-in patterns over the cycle (no streak gamification — pattern is observed, not scored).
- **Manager email engagement:** Open and optional one-tap encouragement rates on biweekly manager status emails.
- **Admin rollup visibility:** Org-wide check-in rate displayed on a single admin view (e.g., "68% weekly check-in rate across N learners this cycle").
- **End-state behavior change (not prototype-measured):** Pre/post assessment delta on the trained skill remains the ultimate success metric; weekly check-ins are a leading indicator only.

## Key Features

### P0 (Must Have - MVP Core)

- **Action plan commitment screen:** Minimal post-training entry point presenting 2–3 curated behavioral guide options plus one AI-labeled suggestion tied to mock assessment results; learner selects one commitment to start the cycle.
- **Onboarding framing message:** Short expectation-setting copy after commitment explaining the weekly one-tap check-in, manager awareness, and growth-mindset tone (no guilt for stalls).
- **Weekly learner check-in email:** Fixed-day prompt — "Did you practice [behavior] this week?" — with three one-tap options (Yes / Partially / Not this week); response logs instantly with no login required.
- **Check-in confirmation with week history chart:** Immediate post-response view (reached via one-tap link from the email) showing a simple dot chart of all elapsed weeks in the cycle — one dot per week, color-coded by response (Yes / Partially / Not / no response). Accompanied by a plain summary (e.g., "4 of 5 weeks reported"). No streak count.
- **Week history chart in emails:** The same dot chart is rendered server-side (e.g., via QuickChart) and embedded in the check-in confirmation view and optionally in subsequent weekly emails, so learners see their pattern without logging in. Chart is generated by querying stored check-in records — no separate streak tracking.
- **Growth-mindset response framing:** Copy after each response reinforcing that progress isn't perfect and misses are useful data, consistent with rewarded vulnerability.
- **Stuck nudge after Partially / Not this week:** Skippable prompt immediately following a Partially or Not this week response — e.g., "Feeling stuck? A quick note to [manager] can help" — so the vulnerable moment is handled rather than dropped. Core to the learner loop, not an optional enhancement.
- **Missed check-in handling:** Explicit rules for weeks with no learner response (see [Missed Check-In Rules](#missed-check-in-rules) below).
- **Biweekly manager status email:** Self-contained email per manager showing each direct report's committed behavior, reporting frequency ("3 of 4 weeks"), next check-in date, plain-text team summary, and server-rendered activity chart image with text fallback.
- **Admin engagement rollup page:** Single view showing org-wide weekly check-in rate aggregated from learner check-in data, with basic breakdown by team/manager where seeded data allows.
- **Demo access without auth:** Role switcher (Learner / Manager / Admin) with pre-populated seed data so reviewers can explore all personas on first load.
- **In-app email preview pages:** Static routes rendering learner check-in and manager report emails exactly as they would appear in an inbox, including the chart.
- **"Try it with your email" live send:** Form to enter an email address and trigger a real send via Resend API, proving end-to-end email delivery. Required for interview demo credibility — in-app previews alone are insufficient to demonstrate a working system.

### P1 (Should Have - Fast Follows)

- **Optional manager encouragement:** One-tap way for managers to send a short reinforcement message from the status email, never required.
- **Re-assessment countdown:** Static message on learner and manager surfaces showing time remaining until the 90-day re-assessment (e.g., "Your re-assessment is in 3 weeks").

### P2 (Nice to Have - Future Scope)

- **Exportable admin report:** Download or copy org engagement summary for L&D buyer presentations.
- **Engagement vs. assessment correlation view:** Overlay check-in rates with re-assessment score deltas once real assessment data is integrated.
- **Custom verified sending domain:** Improved deliverability beyond Resend's default testing domain.
- **Per-skill or per-cohort filtering** on the admin rollup page.
- **Follow-up nudge for missed check-ins:** A gentle reminder when a learner hasn't responded by end of the check-in window (deferred — missed weeks are recorded silently in v1).

## Week History Chart

Each learner sees a simple row of dots — one per elapsed week in the 90-day cycle — rendered from stored check-in records at display time (no precomputed streak or score):

| Week state           | Dot representation                                                                           |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Yes                  | Filled dot (e.g., green)                                                                     |
| Partially            | Half-filled or distinct color (e.g., amber)                                                  |
| Not this week        | Filled dot in a neutral/muted color (e.g., gray) — learner responded honestly, not a failure |
| No response (missed) | Empty/hollow dot or dash                                                                     |

- **Where it appears:** On the confirmation screen immediately after the learner taps a response in their weekly email; optionally embedded in subsequent weekly emails so the pattern stays visible without a dashboard.
- **How it's built:** Query check-in records for the learner's cycle, map each elapsed week to its response state, render via server-side chart image (QuickChart) or inline HTML/CSS for the confirmation page.
- **Summary line:** Plain text alongside the chart — e.g., "4 of 5 weeks reported" — derived from the same query, not a separate metric.
- **No streak tracking:** Consecutive-week counts, streak badges, and streak-break logic are explicitly out of scope. The chart shows the honest pattern; the learner interprets it.

## Missed Check-In Rules

When a learner does not respond to the weekly email by the end of the check-in window (end of the fixed check-in day, or 24 hours after send for prototype simplicity):

- **No response is recorded** for that week — it is not auto-filled as "Not this week."
- **Weeks reported** counts only weeks where the learner submitted any response (Yes, Partially, or Not this week). Display: "3 of 5 weeks reported."
- **Week history chart** shows the missed week as an empty/hollow dot — visible in the pattern, not penalized with a broken streak.
- **Manager and admin views** treat missed weeks as non-reporting: they reduce the reported-weeks ratio shown for each learner (e.g., "2 of 4 weeks"). The plain-text team summary in the manager email and the admin rollup page surface this ratio directly — no computed "falling behind" status label or threshold logic in the prototype. Interpretation is left to the reader.
- **No guilt-based follow-up** for missed weeks in the prototype — the next weekly email simply arrives on schedule. Growth-mindset tone applies only when the learner actively responds.

## User Stories

1. As a **learner** who just finished training, I want to choose one concrete behavioral commitment from curated options or an AI suggestion so that I have a clear, specific practice target for the next 90 days.
2. As a **learner**, I want to report my weekly practice with a single tap in an email so that checking in takes seconds and doesn't require logging into another system.
3. As a **learner**, I want to see a simple chart of which weeks I checked in and which I missed immediately after responding so that I can see my honest pattern without gamified streak pressure.
4. As a **learner** who selected Partially or Not this week, I want an optional prompt to reach out to my manager so that I have a constructive next step when I'm stuck, without being punished for honesty.
5. As a **manager**, I want to receive a biweekly email showing what each direct report committed to and how consistently they've checked in so that I can offer support without needing to log into a dashboard.
6. As a **manager**, I want the option to send a quick encouragement to a direct report so that I can reinforce their effort when I have a moment, without it being a required workflow step.
7. As an **L&D admin**, I want to see an org-wide weekly check-in rate so that I have a concrete engagement metric to demonstrate program value beyond training completion counts.
8. As a **prototype reviewer**, I want to switch between learner, manager, and admin views with realistic seed data, preview emails in-app, and send a live email to my own inbox so that I can verify the system actually works end-to-end.

## Out of Scope

- Streak counts, streak badges, consecutive-week gamification, or streak-break notifications
- Manager accounts, login, or persistent manager dashboards
- Magic-link persistent learner dashboards beyond the immediate check-in confirmation screen
- Calendar integration or configurable check-in day selection
- Multi-step mutual-confirmation loops between manager and learner
- Actual re-assessment scheduling, delivery, or scoring logic
- Real AI-generated commitment suggestions (mock/seeded only for prototype)
- Full LeaderFactor platform integration (assessments, LMS, SSO, facilitator tools)
- Twice-weekly email reminders (replaced entirely by weekly check-in loop)
- Email confirmation/verification flows for the "try it with your email" feature
- Multi-commitment action plans (one behavior per learner per cycle)
- Auto-filling missed weeks as "Not this week" or sending guilt-based chase emails for non-response

## Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk:** Scope exceeds the ~3-hour build window | **Mitigation:** P0 is tight but ordered by demo value — cut P1 (manager encouragement, re-assessment countdown) first; use seed data and text chart fallbacks before polishing chart rendering; Resend live send stays in P0 but can use Resend's default testing domain.
- **Risk:** Email deliverability issues with Resend's default testing domain | **Mitigation:** In-app preview pages guarantee reviewers always see the design; live send proves the pipeline works; document deliverability as a known tradeoff if emails land in spam.
- **Risk:** Chart images blocked by email clients | **Mitigation:** Plain-text status summary above chart serves as mandatory fallback; confirmation page always shows chart via HTML/CSS even if email image is blocked.
- **Risk:** Weekly self-report overstates actual behavior change | **Mitigation:** Position check-ins explicitly as a leading indicator; re-assessment remains the proof metric; growth-mindset copy reduces incentive to always select "Yes."
- **Risk:** Manager awareness without action doesn't improve outcomes | **Mitigation:** Prototype tests the core hypothesis (frictionless reporting + visibility); stuck nudge and optional manager encouragement provide lightweight action paths for v1 validation.
- **Risk:** Admin rollup feels too thin for enterprise buyers | **Mitigation:** Single headline metric ("X% weekly check-in rate") is sufficient for prototype; note cohort filtering and assessment correlation as explicit v2 items.

## Timeline and Milestones

- **Milestone 1 — Core flow (Hour 1):** Seed data, role switcher, commitment screen, onboarding message, weekly check-in UI with one-tap response, confirmation page with week history dot chart, stuck nudge after Partially/Not, missed-week logic.
- **Milestone 2 — Stakeholder surfaces & email (Hour 2):** Manager status email template with commitment details and chart, admin rollup page, in-app email preview routes, server-rendered chart images for emails, Resend live-send integration.
- **Milestone 3 — Deploy & write-up (Hour 3):** Deploy to hosting. P1 items (manager encouragement, re-assessment countdown) only if time allows after deploy is confirmed working. Write-up is a primary deliverable for this milestone, not an afterthought — see below.
