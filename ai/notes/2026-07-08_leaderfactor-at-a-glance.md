# Tell me everything I need to know to understand their product from a product perspective, for the customer, industry, and company regarding LeaderFactor in utah

LeaderFactor is a Utah-based leadership development and training company whose core product is a full‑stack platform for measurable leadership behavior change built around psychological safety and emotional intelligence. From a product perspective, you can think of it as an enterprise “leadership OS” that combines frameworks, assessments, content, in‑workflow tools, analytics, and facilitator certification for HR, L\&D, and People leaders.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_3][^1_4][^1_5]

## Company context

LeaderFactor was founded in 2006 and operates in the professional training and coaching / management development training space (NAICS 611430), with headquarters in the broader Salt Lake City–Lehi corridor of Utah. Multiple data providers estimate it as a small, privately held company with roughly 20–50 employees and annual revenues in the low single‑digit millions. The company was founded by Dr. Timothy R. Clark, an organizational anthropologist and global authority on psychological safety and executive development, who serves as CEO.[^1_6][^1_7][^1_8][^1_3][^1_9][^1_10][^1_1]

## Product architecture

LeaderFactor’s product centers on the “Core15” system: 15 irreducible leadership skills spanning “Lead Self,” “Lead the Team,” and “Lead the Organization,” framed as Heart (being), Head (thinking), and Hands (doing) so expectations are unambiguous. Each skill is anchored in a named, research‑backed framework (e.g., The 4 Stages of Psychological Safety™, The 6 Domains of Emotional Intelligence™, DECIDER Critical Thinking™) paired with a validated assessment index such as PSindex®, EQindex®, DECIDERindex™, CAMindex™, and others. These skills are delivered through a full‑stack platform that integrates six layers—Define, Assess, Learn, Apply, Prove, and Scale—rather than a standalone workshop or content library.[^1_2][^1_4][^1_1]

## Platform and delivery model

The platform experience is structured as one system: shared language for skills (Define), built‑in validated assessments (Assess), cohort and on‑demand learning content (Learn), in‑workflow tools and action plans (Apply), behavior and participation analytics (Prove), and internal facilitator certification for scalability (Scale). Delivery pathways include LeaderFactor’s own experts, certified internal practitioners at the client, or a blended model, with modular 4‑hour workshops that can be split into shorter sessions to match organizational context. Managers and leaders access tools such as conversation guides, decision frameworks, and prompts tied to specific Core15 skills to support high‑stakes moments in the flow of work.[^1_11][^1_4][^1_5][^1_2]

## Customer perspective

The primary customers are HR, L\&D, talent development, OD, and People leaders who need to raise leadership capability across self, team, and organization while proving impact beyond completion rates. The product is tailored to organizations that care about psychological safety, emotional intelligence, change leadership, decision quality, coaching, and team cohesion, and that want data‑driven cultural transformation at scale. Logos cited include large enterprises and institutions such as Cheesecake Factory, AbbVie, Slack, Nespresso, Coca‑Cola, UC Berkeley, Duke, Adobe, Goodyear, and AWS, which signals a focus on mid‑to‑large organizations with complex leadership needs.[^1_8][^1_3][^1_4][^1_5][^1_1][^1_2]

## Jobs‑to‑be‑done for customers

LeaderFactor’s platform is designed to solve several recurring problems for these buyers: fragmented leadership programs assembled from point solutions, lack of measurement and evidence of behavior change, and difficulty connecting training to real work. The JTBD framing is essentially “turn leadership vision into measurable behavior change you can prove,” with LeaderFactor promising pre/post behavioral data at roughly 90 days, cohort analytics by team and dimension, and indices that move beyond sentiment surveys. For managers, the product aims to help them “create conditions where others thrive” by mastering Emotional Intelligence, Psychological Safety, Alignment, Decision Making, and Coaching.[^1_12][^1_4][^1_1][^1_2]

## Industry and competitive landscape

LeaderFactor sits in the professional training and coaching / management development training industry, with formal registration under NAICS 611430 and a positioning around leadership development and cultural transformation. Data providers list competitors that include HR and culture platforms like BambooHR, Culture Amp, Qualtrics, Gallup, Lattice, Betterworks, 15Five, and Peakon, which shows they are perceived as part of a broader HR/L\&D and employee experience ecosystem. Unlike pure survey or engagement platforms, LeaderFactor differentiates on research‑backed leadership frameworks plus integrated assessments and training, rather than only analytics.[^1_7][^1_10][^1_4][^1_1][^1_2][^1_8]

## Measurement and data layer

A core differentiator is the insistence on “evidence over theater,” converting leadership development from a faith‑based investment into something that can be proven to boards and CFOs. Every skill includes a validated index that participants take as a baseline, retake after the program, and re‑measure around 90 days, giving pre/post deltas in behavior at team and org levels. The platform exposes score trajectories, participation data, and gaps between self and external scoring, so development starts from “the truth” rather than self‑reported satisfaction.[^1_4][^1_1][^1_2]

## Security, enterprise readiness, and integrations

LeaderFactor positions the platform as “built for the enterprise,” with SOC 2 Type 2 attestation, annual third‑party penetration testing, encryption at rest and in transit, SAML SSO, tenant isolation, and zero‑data‑retention agreements with AI vendors. This security posture, combined with LMS and SSO support plus admin reporting, is meant to reassure IT and L\&D teams that the platform can be deployed across the whole organization. A trust center with live control status is also referenced, which is typical of SaaS vendors targeting enterprise buyers.[^1_4]

## Company strategy and product fit

Strategically, LeaderFactor is “the company behind The 4 Stages of Psychological Safety™,” using that framework as an anchor brand for broader leadership capability building. Their mission is to “raise the standard of leadership” by building measurable capability at scale across self, team, and organization, aligning identity, thinking, and doing so change sticks. From a company perspective, the product is both a delivery mechanism for Clark’s thought leadership and a scalable system that can move beyond bespoke consulting toward repeatable, platform‑based programs and certifications.[^1_5][^1_1][^1_2]

## Differentiation and strengths (product perspective)

Key differentiation points include: a coherent Core15 skill system, globally known psychological safety framework, validated indices (PSindex®, EQindex®, etc.), and an integrated platform that covers assessment, learning, in‑workflow practice, facilitation, reinforcement, and measurement. The ability to certify internal facilitators, backed by complete facilitator guides, slide decks, participant materials, and platform tooling, helps clients “own delivery” over time instead of depending on external consultants. Their explicit focus on psychological safety and emotional intelligence, plus practical frameworks and scenario‑based learning, is positioned as a way to create real behavioral change rather than one‑off motivational events.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_11][^1_5][^1_4]

## Constraints and product challenges

As a relatively small, Utah‑based firm with <\$5–10M in revenue and a modest employee count, LeaderFactor must compete with much larger HR tech and consulting players for enterprise L\&D budgets. Their product depends on customers valuing validated behavioral assessments and structured frameworks enough to displace or complement existing LMS content libraries, survey tools, and generic leadership programs, which can be an adoption hurdle in organizations used to piecemeal solutions. They also operate in a crowded space where many vendors now claim to address psychological safety, engagement, and leadership, so continued differentiation will hinge on outcomes data, platform UX, and integration into customers’ broader people‑systems stack.[^1_3][^1_10][^1_7][^1_2][^1_1][^1_4]
